Research & Documentation

Methods, Data & Open Questions

This page documents the data sources, statistical models, and design decisions behind the Conflicts Dashboard, and lists open research directions for extending the project.

COW Dataset

The dashboard draws on two datasets maintained by the Correlates of War (COW) Project, a long-running scientific effort to identify and measure the correlates of war across recorded history.

Dataset Version Coverage Description
Inter-State War Dataset v4.0 1823–2010 Records of militarised inter-state wars, participants, outcomes, and battle deaths.
National Material Capabilities v6.0 1816–2016 Annual estimates of six military-industrial-demographic capability components for each state.

War outcome coding follows COW conventions: a state is recorded as Winner, Loser, or Draw based on whether it achieved its primary war aims. The dashboard includes 91 inter-state wars and 92 belligerent states over the combined period 1823–2010.

NMC Components

The National Material Capabilities index decomposes state power into six annual components. The dashboard uses the ratio of each component between the initiator and the target state as model inputs.

Component Abbreviation Concept
Military Personnel milper Total armed forces personnel (thousands)
Military Expenditure milex Military spending in constant USD
Iron & Steel Production irst Industrial war-making capacity (thousands of tonnes)
Energy Consumption pec Primary energy consumption (coal-tonne equivalents)
Total Population tpop Total population (thousands)
Urban Population upop Population in cities >25,000 (thousands)
Composite Index (CINC) The Composite Index of National Capability (CINC) score is each state's share of the total world capability across all six components in a given year. It appears in the dashboard but is not used directly as a model predictor — individual component ratios are used instead to preserve signal.

Bayesian Beta-Binomial Model

The dashboard fits a Beta-Binomial conjugate model to the observed win/loss record of each country-pair. This allows the user to start with prior beliefs about the win probability and update those beliefs sequentially as conflicts are observed.

Prior:     P(Win) ~ Beta(α, β)
Update:   Posterior = Beta(α + wins, β + losses)
α = prior wins  |  β = prior losses  |  95% HDI via Beta quantiles

The prior parameters α and β are exposed as sliders in the sidebar. Setting α = β = 1 produces a flat (uniform) prior, expressing complete prior ignorance. Increasing either parameter expresses a stronger prior belief in favour of wins or losses respectively.

The sequential trace chart shows how the posterior mean moves after each war in the selected country-pair's history, illustrating how evidence accumulates and how sensitive the final estimate is to the starting prior.

Logistic Regression Model

A logistic regression model is trained on five of the six NMC component ratios to predict binary war outcome (Win vs. Loss; draws are excluded). Inputs are standardised (zero mean, unit variance) before fitting.

P(Win) = σ(w·X + b)
X = standardised log-ratios of 5 NMC components  |  σ = logistic function
AUC = 0.790 on out-of-sample conflicts

The model achieves an AUC of 0.790 on held-out conflicts, meaning it correctly ranks a randomly chosen winner above a randomly chosen loser ~79% of the time. This is a meaningful but imperfect signal — capability alone does not determine war outcomes.

Caveat Logistic regression captures linear relationships in capability space. Non-linearities (e.g., nuclear deterrence thresholds, alliance effects, terrain) are not modelled. The AUC reflects the predictive power of material capability data alone, not a complete theory of war.

Setting Priors

The prior panel lets you inject domain knowledge before observing any data. A few useful starting points:

Prior Type α β Interpretation
Uniform / ignorant 1 1 No prior knowledge; posterior is driven entirely by data.
Slight win-bias 2 1 Express a weak belief that initiators win slightly more often.
Historical base-rate ~1.4 ~1 Encode the COW-wide initiator win rate of ~58% as a prior.
Skeptical 5 5 Strong prior toward 50/50; requires substantial data to move.

Country Pairs

The dashboard shows all conflicts in which both the selected initiator and target appear in the COW dataset. Each conflict card shows:

— War name, year, and initiator outcome
— The opposing state and its role
— Bar charts for all six NMC ratios at the time of conflict
— The logistic model's predicted win probability given those ratios

Selecting different country-pairs re-runs both the Bayesian update sequence and the capability charts. The stats strip at the top of the dashboard updates to reflect the new country-pair's historical record.

Research Ideas

The following are open directions for extending this project, organised roughly by difficulty.

  • 01
    Alliance Effects
    Incorporate COW Formal Alliance Dataset to model how alliance portfolios change predicted win probabilities. Does the capability of allies significantly improve AUC beyond bilateral NMC ratios alone?
  • 02
    Temporal Drift in Component Weights
    Fit the logistic model on rolling 40-year windows to test whether the predictive weight of military expenditure vs. industrial capacity shifts across historical eras (e.g., pre-WWI industrialisation vs. Cold War).
  • 03
    Hierarchical Bayesian Model by Region
    Replace the flat Beta-Binomial with a hierarchical model that shares information across regional country-pairs (e.g., European, Middle Eastern, East Asian dyads). This would partially pool estimates to reduce variance for data-sparse pairs.
  • 04
    Draws as a Separate Outcome
    The current model treats draws as missing data. Extending to a Dirichlet-Multinomial model (Win / Draw / Loss) would let the dashboard estimate draw probability alongside win probability and visualise the full outcome simplex.
  • 05
    Nuclear Threshold Indicator
    Overlay a binary nuclear-state flag on the NMC ratio charts. Test whether nuclear status is a statistically significant predictor of war outcomes in the logistic model, and whether it moderates the capability ratio effect.
  • 06
    Duration and Casualty Prediction
    Extend beyond win/loss binary outcomes to model war duration and battle deaths as separate targets. NMC ratios may predict magnitude of conflict differently than direction of outcome.
  • 07
    Live Capability Estimates
    The COW NMC dataset ends at 2010. Sourcing post-2010 capability data from SIPRI, World Bank, and IISS would allow the dashboard to produce current-year estimates rather than relying on extrapolation from the last known year.
